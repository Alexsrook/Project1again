Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park & Mausoleums

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah is hailed as one of the world's most prominent African leaders. On March 6th 1957, he led Ghana to gain independence from its British colonial master. The Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park and Mausoleum was dedicated to Dr. Kwame Nkrumah in 1992 to commemorate his life and work after his death. Located in Accra, the capital of Ghana, the mausoleum is the final resting place of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah and his wife, Fatia Nkrumah. You can also find rare artifacts related to Ghana's independence here. A visit to the Kwame n'Kluma Mausoleum and Memorial Park will inspire you to understand how Ghana gained independence from its in-depth history. It is highly recommended for you to visit!




Mole National Park

Another place to remember when you visit Ghana, is the northern region. Located in northern Ghana, Mole Rat National Park is home to endangered species such as elephants, Roan antelopes and some rare birds. Since the land area of this national park is 4,840 square kilometers, it is one of the best places to visit when you want to explore nature. This park is the first park in Ghana to be reserved for wildlife conservation. It protects more than 300 species of birds, 94 species of mammals and several other species of wildlife.




Paga Crocodile Pond

Most of us do not know much about crocodiles as they either scare us or we do not want to meet them. But this sanctuary is made for hosting the crocodiles and the enthusiasts can see many of them over here. The crocodiles residing over here are actually very friendly towards humans that visit them. Tourists can feed the crocodiles and get up close and personal with them. This place respects and honors their crocodiles and is against hurting any of them. Guides are always present to break tourists unnecessary fear of crocodiles. A great place to visit if you wish to try something exciting!
